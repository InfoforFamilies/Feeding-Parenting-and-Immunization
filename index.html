Queensway Carleton Hospital

Well baby, Jaundice and Breastfeeding Clinic

QCH sees babies in their clinic after your baby goes home. For the first follow up visit, if you do not have a family doctor a you may get booked into their well baby clinic where your baby can be assessed for weight, infant feeding and checked by a doctor. If there is a concern about jaundice, the doctor can book a follow up visit.

QCH offers feeding support for families as well for babies up to 3 weeks after the baby is discharged.

Book an appointment before you go home call to book at 613-721-2000 x4816

Ottawa Public Health

For continued follow up, families that may not have access to a family doctor, Ottawa Public Health (OPH) is helping to provide free support.

OPH offers a variety of services from Covid-19 and flu vaccines, to dental screening, parenting and lactation support and immunizations for school age children and now there are some hubs offering routine vaccinations for children.

These hubs are drop-in and therefore no appointment is needed.

Feeding and Parenting Support

These are staffed by a public health nurse and lactation consultant. You can get support with infant feeding, your baby can be weighed, learn ways for your child to be healthy and active, check in with your baby’s growth and development, social and emotional development. These are for families with children 0-6 years old.

Parenting in Ottawa Drop-in Clinics

The Parenting In Ottawa drop-ins are located across the City of Ottawa.

Speak, in person, with a Public Health Nurse or lactation consultant about questions that you might have.

  • Get support for infant feeding

  • Breast pumps are available to borrow for 1-2 weeks

  • Make sure your child is on track with their growth and development

  • Provides support and information about how to take care of your baby

The program is Free and available to:

  • Expectant parents,

  • Parents and guardians of children aged birth to 6 years of age

Routine Immunizations for Babies and Children

  • Children require immunizations at 2, 4, 6, 12, 15 and 18 months

  • Children then require immunizations between 4 years and 6 years of age

  • These immunization clinics are for families who do not have OHIP or a family doctor.

  • If pregnant can get Tdap (Tetanus, Diptheria and Pertussis) between 28-32 weeks

  • Please bring any immunization records you already have and some kind of identification for you and your children (if available)

For more information on publicly funded immunizations (free) see Vaccines through the lifespan
